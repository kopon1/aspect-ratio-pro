What is Aspect Ratio?

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between the width and height of an image or screen. It's typically expressed as two numbers separated by a colon (e.g., 16:9).

Understanding Aspect Ratio

For example, a 16:9 aspect ratio means:

  • For every 16 units of width, there are 9 units of height
  • Common resolutions include: 1920×1080, 1280×720, 3840×2160
  • Most modern displays and video content use this ratio

Why Aspect Ratio Matters

  • Photography: Affects composition and print sizes
  • Video Production: Determines how content will display on different screens
  • Web Design: Crucial for responsive images and layouts
  • Gaming: Impacts field of view and display compatibility

How to Calculate Aspect Ratio

  1. Start with the width and height measurements
  2. Divide both numbers by their greatest common divisor (GCD)
  3. The resulting numbers form your aspect ratio

Example: 1920×1080

GCD of 1920 and 1080 is 120

1920 ÷ 120 = 16

1080 ÷ 120 = 9

Therefore, the aspect ratio is 16:9
